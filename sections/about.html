About

Hi! I am a PhD student in Computer Science from the University of Buenos Aires, with a deep interest in researching and developing on what is called neuroscience-inspired artificial intelligence. This past decade has seen a surge in neuroscience research and, as more and more data are gathered, the collaboration between these two historically intertwined disciplines will only become more relevant.

I am particularly interested in the use of eye-tracking, for its accessibility and potential for scalability, and in (f)MRI, for its unparalleled precision in mapping the structure and activity of the brain. By developing machine learning models that are able to incorporate such data, I aim to contribute to the understanding of abstract semantic representations in the brain and the cognitive algorithms that underpin them.

In addition to these exciting projects, I continue to be actively involved in the maintenance of a benchmark for the task of visual search in natural scenes, which stemmed from my MSc thesis.
