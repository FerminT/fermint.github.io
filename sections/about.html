About

Hi! I am a PhD student in Computer Science at the Universidad de Buenos Aires, with a deep interest in researching and developing at the intersection of neuroscience and artificial intelligence. This past decade has seen a surge in neuroscience research and, as more and more data are collected, the collaboration between these two historically intertwined disciplines will only become more relevant.

I am particularly interested in the use of (f)MRI, for its unparalleled precision in mapping the structure and activity of the brain, and eye-tracking, for its accessibility and potential for scalability. Through the development of machine learning models that are able to incorporate such data, I aim to create abstract representations of the brain that help us answer deep questions regarding its structure and functionality.

In addition to these exciting projects, I continue to be actively involved in the maintenance of a benchmark for the task of visual search in natural scenes, which stemmed from my MSc thesis.
